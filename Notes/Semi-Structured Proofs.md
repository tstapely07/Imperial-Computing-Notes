In our [[40018 - Discrete Mathematics, Logic, and Reasoning|reasoning]] proofs, we will adopt a **semi-structured** style.
* This allows us to ensure our argument remains clear, but without the lengthy boilerplate of **natural deduction**.

In a **semi-structured proof**, we first state the **givens**.
* We then state what needs to be shown.
* Then we construct our proof line by line, justifying every line as we go.
* Once we have reached and justified what needed to be shown, we have completed our proof.
