> [!Axiom]
> Every **non-empty subset** $A\subseteq \mathbb{R}$ with an **upper bound** has a [[Supremum and Infimum|supremum]] in $\mathbb{R}$.

This seems trivial, but for a **set** such as the **rational numbers** ($\mathbb{Q}$), the **supremum** is not necessarily in the **set**.
* For example, the subset $A = \{x \in \mathbb{Q} : x^2 < 2\}$ is clearly **bounded** above, but the **supremum** is $\sqrt{2}$, which is not in $Q$.
