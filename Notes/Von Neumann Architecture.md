**Von Neumann architecture** is the simplest description of a **general-purpose computer**:
* ![[Pasted image 20260415110201.png|601]]
Both **programs** and **data** are stored in **memory**.
* In fact, this **storage** of **programs** is what makes the **computer** **general-purpose**, as supplying different **programs** allows it to perform different **computations**.
