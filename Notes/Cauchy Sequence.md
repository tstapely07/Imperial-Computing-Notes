> [!Abstract] Definition
> A [[Sequences|sequence]] $(a_n)_{n\ge1}$ is called a **Cauchy sequence** if for every $\epsilon\gt 0$, there exists an integer $N$ such that for all $m,n\gt N$:
> $$|a_n-a_m|\lt\epsilon$$

Essentially, instead of the **terms** tending to some [[Limits|limit]] $L$, the distance between any two **terms** just becomes infinitely small.
