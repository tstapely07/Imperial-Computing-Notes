Let $\vec{a} = \begin{bmatrix}a_1\\a_2\\\vdots\\a_n\end{bmatrix}$, and $\vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix}b_1\\b_2\\\vdots\\b_n\end{bmatrix}$.
The **inner product** is the **row** [[Vectors|vector]] times the **column vector**, and results in a **scalar**:
$$\vec{a}\cdot \vec{b}=\vec{a}^\top \vec{b}=\vec{b}^\top \vec{a}=\begin{bmatrix}a_1&a_2&\dots&a_n\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}b_1\\b_2\\\vdots\\b_n\end{bmatrix}=|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\cos\theta=\sum_{i=1}^na_ib_i$$
Here, $\theta$ is the angle between the two **vectors**.
* Two vectors are **orthogonal** iff their inner product is **zero**.
* $a\perp b\iff a\cdot b=0$

> [!Note]
> The **inner product** is also known as the **dot product**.
