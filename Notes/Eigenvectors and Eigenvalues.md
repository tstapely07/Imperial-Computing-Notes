*Consider* a **square** [[Matrices|matrix]] $A_{n\times n}$.
The **eigenvectors** are the [[vectors]] which do not change **direction** when [[Linear Transformations|transformed]] by $A$.
* They are only **scaled**.
If $\vec{x}$ is an **eigenvector** of $A$, then:
$$A\vec{x}=\lambda\vec{x}$$
* We call $\lambda$ the **eigenvalue** of the **eigenvector** $\vec{x}$.
If $\vec{y}$ is an **eigenvector**, then any scalar multiple $\alpha\vec{y}$ is also an **eigenvector** with the **same eigenvalue**.
The **spectrum** of $A$, denoted $\operatorname{spectrum}(A)$, is the set of all distinct **eigenvalues**, $\lambda_1,\dots,\lambda_n$.
