A **linear mapping** is a **function** across [[Vector Spaces|vector spaces]] which preserves the [[Vectors|vector]] **addition** and **scalar multiplication** across the **spaces**:
$$\begin{gather}f:\left(V,\oplus_V,\otimes_V,\overrightarrow{0_V}\right)\longrightarrow\left(W,\oplus_W,\otimes_W,\overrightarrow{0_W}\right)\\
\text{Such that }f(\overrightarrow{a}\oplus_V\overrightarrow{b})=f(\overrightarrow{a})\oplus_W f(\overrightarrow{b})\text{ and }f(\lambda\otimes_V\overrightarrow{a})=\lambda\otimes_Wf(\overrightarrow{a})
\end{gather}$$

> [!Note]
> Another useful property we can deduce from the previous two is that:
> $$f(\overrightarrow{0_V})=\overrightarrow{0_w}$$
