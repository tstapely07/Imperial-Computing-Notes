> [!Abstract] Definition
> **Relations** are sets of typed tuples.
> They take the form $R(A,B,\dots)$ where $R$ is the name of the **relation**.
> 

> [!Abstract] Definition
> The number of [[attributes]] $n$ is the **arity** of the **relation**.
> * $R(A_1,\dots,A_n)$ is an $n$-ary **relation**.

> [!Abstract] Definition
> The **extent** of $R(A,B,\dots)$ is the **set** of **tuples**:
> $$\{\langle v_{1}^{A},v_{1}^{B},...\rangle, \langle v_{2}^{A},v_{2}^{B},...\rangle, \langle v_{3}^{A},v_{3}^{B},...\rangle, ... \}$$

> [!Abstract] Definition
> The **cardinality** of a **relation**, $|R|$ is the number of **tuples** in the **extent** of $R$.

Rules of **relations**:
* There must be no duplicate **tuples**.
* The order of **tuples** does not matter.
* The order of **attributes** does not matter.
* All **tuples** must have the same **arity**.

A relation is analogous to a table:
* ![[Pasted image 20260405103243.png|232]]
* Each **attribute** is a **column**.
* Each **tuple** is a **row**.
